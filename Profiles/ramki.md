# Ramki Profile

Last updated: 2026-05-22

## Practical Read

Ramki needs clear operating visibility, not a long narrative. For project updates, he should be able to understand the situation in one screen and quickly decide whether he needs to engage.

The EON update feedback is an important calibration point: the first version was too verbose. The better version was a short executive summary, a compact milestone table, and only the strongest current risks/decisions.

## What He Cares About

- Current project health: RAG, milestone, ETA, blocker.
- Customer/bank impact and production readiness.
- Whether the team has a real launch path or needs rebaseline.
- External or cross-team dependencies that need escalation.
- Effort and spend signal, but only at a directional level unless actual cost data is available.
- Clear evidence from Jira, Confluence, release notes, or live blockers.

## Preferred Update Style

- Single-screen view.
- Lead with the conclusion.
- Use a compact table for milestones.
- Keep risks to the top 3-5.
- Link evidence, but do not over-explain it.
- Ask Ramki only for decisions, escalations, or sponsorship he is actually needed for.
- Mark missing data plainly instead of filling gaps with assumptions.

## Good Format

```text
Status: Red / Amber / Green
Summary: <one or two sentences>

| Milestone | RAG | ETA | Status | Ask / Decision |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Top risks:
1. <risk> - <evidence link> - <decision/owner if needed>
2. <risk> - <evidence link> - <decision/owner if needed>
```

## Questions To Anticipate

- Is this on track?
- What changed since the last checkpoint?
- What is the real blocker?
- What is the customer, bank, or platform impact?
- What is the decision needed now?
- Who owns the next action?
- What is the evidence?
- What date should I believe?

## Avoid

- Long background sections.
- Large ticket dumps.
- Too many asks.
- Explaining decisions Deepak can make himself.
- Treating every open item as a Ramki escalation.
- Currency spend estimates without actual rate or allocation data.
