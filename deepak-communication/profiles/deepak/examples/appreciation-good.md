# Appreciation Good Example

Synthetic examples. Replace or supplement these with real messages written or approved by Deepak.

## Compact Example

The release readiness review moved forward today with the main blockers now closed.

Anita tightened the open validation items and confirmed the remaining edge cases. Rohan worked through the deployment issue and got the fix verified without adding risk to the release. Priya kept Product, Support and Engineering aligned so decisions did not stall while the team was debugging.

That combination of ownership, coordination and calm execution helped us protect the release timeline and avoid a noisy handoff. Thank you to everyone who helped get this over the line.

## Why This Works

- Specific about the progress and why it mattered.
- Names people only for concrete work.
- Warm without exaggeration.
- Short enough for a Teams-style appreciation note.

## Expanded Workshop Example

Thank you Anita, Rohan, Priya, Dev and Sameer for leaning in through the two-day workshop.

Anita, thanks for anchoring both the prep and the session at short notice, and making it work well. This is becoming a good habit, and it is exactly what we need.

Rohan, the strategy document came out well. Most of the topics that came up in the room were already addressed in the doc in one form or another, so it became a useful way to keep the conversation moving and drive better follow-up questions.

Priya, your grasp of the operational flow and the technical footprint helped set the tone for the rest of the conversation. It gave us the base from which we could steer the workshop with confidence.

Dev, your sharp and specific interventions helped both the prep and the actual session move in the right direction.

Sameer, good work getting the material together and keeping the quality bar high. We started with ambiguity, and the preparatory doc helped create the clarity needed for the discussion.

Thank you all. It was good to see the team cracking this together with minimal intervention from outside this small group. This gives us a solid base for the next round of discussions and sets a good pattern for similar engagements in future.

## Why This Works

- Uses one short paragraph per person when the work deserves it.
- Recognises preparation, artefacts, domain command, interventions and quality.
- Describes the effect of each contribution on the workshop.
- Warm and personal without sounding ceremonial.
- Ends with why the effort matters for the next round and future engagements.
