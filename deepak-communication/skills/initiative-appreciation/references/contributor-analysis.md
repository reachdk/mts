# Contributor Analysis

## Initiative

Determine:

- What was being attempted?
- What problem was being solved?
- What outcome, decision, milestone or progress occurred?
- Why did it matter?

## Contributors

For every possible contributor, capture:

- Name
- Observable action
- Result of the action
- Evidence source: browser-visible, user-supplied, or inferred
- Evidence from the conversation or the user's instruction
- Confidence: high, medium or low
- Suggested treatment: name directly, recognise as part of a group, or omit

Include people who visibly:

- Solved an important problem.
- Took ownership.
- Coordinated people or teams.
- Made an important decision.
- Unblocked delivery.
- Completed meaningful work.
- Found or prevented a risk.
- Provided critical analysis.
- Responded effectively during an incident.
- Helped bring the initiative to closure.
- Anchored preparation before an important session.
- Created, improved, or quality-checked an artefact that shaped the discussion.
- Turned ambiguity into clearer structure, questions, options, or a plan.
- Demonstrated domain or technical command that gave the room confidence.
- Steered a live discussion back to useful outcomes.
- Coached the team during preparation or execution.

When recognising artefacts, describe the effect:

- Did the document keep the conversation moving?
- Did it help generate better follow-up questions?
- Did it create a shared frame for the workshop or review?
- Did it improve quality, clarity, or confidence?

Do not include someone merely because:

- They sent many messages.
- They acknowledged a message.
- They were tagged.
- They attended a meeting.
- They are senior.
- They assigned the work.
- They asked for a status update.
- Their name appeared in the thread.

Distinguish:

- Primary contributors.
- Supporting contributors.
- Leadership or sponsorship.
- Informational participants.
- Uncertain contributors.

User-supplied context is valid evidence. Mark it as user-supplied in the attribution check when it is not visible in the browser conversation. Do not treat it as less useful, but avoid presenting it as browser-visible evidence.

Do not give a sponsor credit for work performed by the delivery team.

Do not give the person announcing the result credit for producing the result unless the conversation supports it.
